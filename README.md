### Abstract

We derive and validate a two-regime model of galactic orbital dynamics. The critical acceleration threshold separating both regimes is

$$g_{\rm crit} = \frac{cH_0}{4\pi^2\sqrt{3}} \approx 9.58 \times 10^{-12}\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}$$

derived without free parameters from three independent sources: Fourier orthogonality on the De Sitter correlation time (fixing $4\pi^2$), the Beltrami-Laplace spectrum of the horizon section $S^3$ and the Lichnerowicz-Dirac identity on $S^4$ (both independently fixing $\sqrt{3}$). Starting from the $k$-essence Lagrangian $\mathcal{L} = -\tfrac{1}{3}g_{\rm crit}^2 X^{3/2} - 4\pi G\rho\phi$, with $X = (\nabla\phi)^2/g_{\rm crit}^2$, Euler-Lagrange variation yields the field equation $\nabla\cdot[(|\nabla\phi|/g_{\rm crit})\nabla\phi] = 4\pi G\rho$, whose spherically symmetric solution gives $\phi'(r) = \sqrt{GM\,g_{\rm crit}}/r$ and orbital velocity $V^4 = GM_{\rm bar}\,g_{\rm crit}$ exactly. The mode $m=1$ is selected by a dual falsification test: only $m=1$ satisfies both median velocity error $<20\%$ in collective galaxies (median $10.0\%$, $5/5$ below threshold) and systematic failure $\sim\!55\%$ in Hamiltonian galaxies across 175 SPARC and 9 LITTLE THINGS galaxies. Modes $m\geq 2$ are excluded observationally (medians $34\%$ and $46\%$, respectively). Since $g_{\rm crit}(z) = cH(z)/(4\pi^2\sqrt{3})$, the Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation evolves as $V(z)/V(0) = [H(z)/H_0]^{1/4}$, predicting a universal $+0.120\,\mathrm{dex}$ shift at $z=2$, independent of baryonic mass, falsifiable with JWST and ELT. Zero free parameters throughout.
