Abstract

We derive and validate a two-regime model of galactic orbital dynamics. The critical acceleration threshold separating both regimes is

\[ g_\mathrm{crit} = \frac{cH_0}{4\pi^2\sqrt{3}} \approx 9.58 \times 10^{-12}\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-2}} \]

derived without free parameters from three independent geometric sources. Starting from a \(k\)-essence Lagrangian, Euler-Lagrange variation yields \(V^4 = GM_\mathrm{bar}\,g_\mathrm{crit}\) exactly. A quantum derivation is provided: the Bunch-Davies Wightman propagator, via the Feynman-Vernon influence functional, yields an effective Drude equation \(m\ddot{x}+\gamma\dot{x}=-\nabla V\) with \(\gamma = H_0/(4\pi^2)\), giving \(g_\mathrm{crit} = \gamma c/\sqrt{3}\) in closed form. The flat rotation curve (plateau) is a topological consequence of the compact \(S^3\) horizon geometry. Mode \(m=1\) is confirmed by a dual falsification test across 184 galaxies. The BTFR evolves as \(V(z)/V(0) = [H(z)/H_0]^{1/4}\), predicting \(+0.120\,\mathrm{dex}\) at \(z=2\), falsifiable with JWST and ELT. Zero free parameters throughout.

Introduction

The flat rotation curves of dwarf and low-surface-brightness galaxies have resisted a first-principles explanation since Rubin et al. (1980). Standard Newtonian gravity predicts \(V \propto R^{-1/2}\); observations yield \(V \approx \mathrm{const}\). The canonical resolution invokes a dark matter halo, but the empirical relation \(V^4 \propto M_\mathrm{bar}\) — the Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation (BTFR; McGaugh 2000) — remains unaccounted for within \(\Lambda\)CDM.

Modified Newtonian Dynamics (MOND; Milgrom 1983) reproduces the BTFR by postulating \(a_0 \approx 1.2\times10^{-10}\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}\), but provides no derivation. Note that \(a_0/g_\mathrm{crit} \approx 12.5\): these are distinct scales; the present work is not a reformulation of MOND.

This paper presents a framework in which: (1) \(g_\mathrm{crit}\) is derived, not postulated; (2) an independent quantum derivation from the Bunch-Davies vacuum confirms the same value; (3) the flat rotation curve is interpreted topologically; (4) all predictions are explicitly falsifiable with zero free parameters.

Field Equation and Equation of State

2.1 Explicit Hypotheses

Hypothesis 1 — Fundamental Mode
The orbital mode coupling to the De Sitter horizon is \(m=1\). Motivated by the Maupertuis principle (§5) and confirmed observationally (§6).
Hypothesis 2 — Collective-Regime Lagrangian
In the deep collective regime \((|\nabla\phi|/g_\mathrm{crit} \ll 1)\):
\[ \mathcal{L} = -\frac{g_\mathrm{crit}^2}{3}X^{3/2} - 4\pi G\rho\phi, \quad X = \frac{(\nabla\phi)^2}{g_\mathrm{crit}^2} \]
\(F(X)=X^{3/2}\) is the unique \(k\)-essence function consistent with \(\mu(x)\sim x\) as \(x\to0\) (Bekenstein & Milgrom 1984).
Hypothesis 3 — Integration Constant
\(C=0\) in the exterior solution, from continuity of \(\phi'\) at \(R_T\).

2.2 Euler-Lagrange Derivation

Varying with respect to \(\phi\), the prefactor cancels exactly: \(\tfrac{g_\mathrm{crit}^2}{3}\cdot\tfrac{3}{2}\cdot\tfrac{2}{g_\mathrm{crit}^2}=1\). The field equation is AQUAL in the deep regime \(\mu(x)=x\):

\[ \nabla\cdot\!\left[\frac{|\nabla\phi|}{g_\mathrm{crit}}\nabla\phi\right] = 4\pi G\rho \qquad (1) \]

2.3 Spherical Symmetry and Equation of State

Integrating over a sphere with \(C=0\) (from matching at \(R_T=\sqrt{GM_\mathrm{bar}/g_\mathrm{crit}}\)) and applying the circular orbit condition \(V^2/r=\phi'(r)\):

\[ \boxed{V^4 = G M_\mathrm{bar}\,g_\mathrm{crit}}, \qquad R_T = \sqrt{\frac{GM_\mathrm{bar}}{g_\mathrm{crit}}} \qquad (2) \]

Every step from Hypothesis 2 to equation (2) is algebraic; no additional assumptions are required.

Spectral Geometry: Derivation of \(g_\mathrm{crit}\)

3.1 The Factor \(4\pi^2\)

The De Sitter horizon has Gibbons-Hawking temperature \(T_\mathrm{GH}=\hbar H_0/(2\pi k_B)\) (Gibbons & Hawking 1977), defining \(t_\mathrm{corr}=1/H_0\). Only modes \(\Omega=2\pi m H_0\) couple non-trivially. For \(m=1\), the centripetal acceleration at \(R_H=c/H_0\) is \(a_H^{(1)}=4\pi^2 cH_0\). Independently, \(4\pi^2 = 2\cdot\mathrm{Vol}(S^3) = 2\cdot2\pi^2\), encoding the four-dimensionality of De Sitter spacetime.

3.2 The Factor \(\sqrt{3}\): Three Independent Routes

Route 1. \(\lambda_1^{S^3}=l(l+2)|_{l=1}=3\Rightarrow\sqrt{3}\).

Route 2. Lichnerowicz-Weitzenböck on \(S^4\): \(\not{D}^2=-\Box+R/4\), \(R=12\), \(\mu_0=3\Rightarrow\sqrt{3}\).

Route 3. Isotropy: \(\langle a_r^2\rangle=\langle a^2\rangle/3\Rightarrow a_r=a/\sqrt{3}\).

All three converge: \(\sqrt{3}=\sqrt{\lambda_1^{S^3}}=\sqrt{n_\mathrm{dim}}\). This means \(g_\mathrm{crit}\) encodes the spatial dimensionality of the universe; the observed value confirms \(n=3\).

\[ \boxed{g_\mathrm{crit} = \frac{cH_0}{4\pi^2\sqrt{3}} = \frac{cH_0}{2\cdot\mathrm{Vol}(S^3)\cdot\sqrt{\lambda_1^{S^3}}} \approx 9.58\times10^{-12}\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}} \qquad (3) \]

Quantum Derivation from the Bunch-Davies Vacuum

4.1 Vacuum Resolution Principle

Principle — Vacuum Resolution Principle

A classical system embedded in the De Sitter vacuum undergoes a dynamical regime transition when its characteristic dynamical timescale reaches the vacuum correlation timescale: \(t_\mathrm{dyn} = t_\mathrm{corr} = 1/H_0\). This fixes the critical orbital frequency \(\omega_\mathrm{crit}=2\pi H_0\).

4.2 Reduced Density Matrix

The full system: \(S_\mathrm{total}=S_\mathrm{orb}[x]+S_\mathrm{BD}[\phi]+S_\mathrm{int}[x,\phi]\). The reduced density matrix:

\[ \rho_\mathrm{red} = \mathrm{Tr}_\mathrm{BD}\!\left[e^{iS_\mathrm{total}}\right] = e^{i(S_\mathrm{orb}+S_\mathrm{IF})} \]

The Bunch-Davies Wightman propagator and its low-frequency spectral density:

\[ G^+(\tau) = \frac{H_0^2}{4\pi^2}e^{-H_0|\tau|}, \qquad \tilde{K}(\omega\to0) = \frac{H_0}{4\pi^2} \equiv \gamma \]

Variation \(\delta S_\mathrm{eff}/\delta x=0\) yields the exact system (no approximations):

\[ m\ddot{x} + \frac{H_0^2}{4\pi^2}Q = -\nabla V, \qquad \dot{Q} = \dot{x} - H_0 Q \qquad (4) \]

4.3 Effective Dissipation

With ansatz \(x(t)=R\cos\omega t\), the effective dissipation coefficient:

\[ \gamma(\omega) = \frac{H_0}{4\pi^2}\cdot\frac{1}{1+(\omega/H_0)^2} \qquad (5) \]

The system selects \(\omega=H_0\). For a 2D circular orbit: \(\gamma_\mathrm{eff}^{2D}=H_0/(4\pi^2)=\gamma\).

4.4 Separation Theorem

Theorem — Separation Theorem

\(g_\mathrm{crit} = S \times K\) where \(S=cH_0\) (scale factor, cosmological, evolves as \(cH(z)\)) and \(K=1/(4\pi^2\sqrt{3})\) (kernel factor, dimensionless, universal, constant). \[ g_\mathrm{crit} = \gamma\cdot\frac{c}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{H_0}{4\pi^2}\cdot\frac{c}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{cH_0}{4\pi^2\sqrt{3}} \qquad (6) \]

Equations (3) and (6) are derived by entirely independent routes and agree exactly. This cross-validation is a non-trivial internal consistency test.

4.5 Plateau as \(S^3\) Topology

The De Sitter horizon section \(S^3\) is compact and without boundary. Any geodesic in \(S^3\) returns to its origin. When \(g < g_\mathrm{crit}\), the orbital dynamics couples to this geometry: the system lives in a compact space where escape velocity does not exist. The flat rotation curve (plateau) is therefore a topological consequence — not a dark matter halo, not a modification of gravity.

4.6 Scale Symmetry Breaking

The collective potential \(V_C=-V_0^2\ln r\) is dynamically invariant under \(r\to\lambda r\); the Hamiltonian potential \(V_H=-GM/r\) is not. Define the order parameter \(\Phi=g/g_\mathrm{crit}-1\). The transition at \(\Phi=0\) marks the breaking of scale symmetry by mass \(M\), introducing the scale \(R_T=\sqrt{GM/g_\mathrm{crit}}\).

Conclusion

We have derived \(V^4=GM_\mathrm{bar}\,g_\mathrm{crit}\) from a \(k\)-essence Lagrangian with every algebraic step exact. The critical acceleration \(g_\mathrm{crit}=cH_0/(4\pi^2\sqrt{3})\) is fixed without free parameters by three independent geometric routes and confirmed by an independent quantum derivation via the Bunch-Davies influence functional: \(m\ddot{x}+\gamma\dot{x}=-\nabla V\) with \(\gamma=H_0/(4\pi^2)\), from which \(g_\mathrm{crit}=\gamma c/\sqrt{3}\) follows in closed form. The agreement of two independent derivations is a non-trivial internal consistency test.

The flat rotation curve is a topological consequence of the compact \(S^3\) horizon geometry. The transition is a breaking of scale symmetry by gravitational mass, with order parameter \(\Phi=g/g_\mathrm{crit}-1\). The single non-trivial hypothesis (\(m=1\)) is confirmed by a dual falsification test across 184 galaxies with zero free parameters. The BTFR is a local manifestation of De Sitter horizon physics, evolving as \(H(z)^{1/4}\), predicting \(+0.120\pm0.030\) dex at \(z=2\), universal, mass-independent, and testable with JWST and ELT.
